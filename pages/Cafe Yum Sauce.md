- ## Ingredients
-
- 3/4 cup [[chickpea]] (1/2 can), or 2.5 oz [[chickpea flour]]
- 1/2 tsp [[baking soda]] (omit if using chickpea flour)
- 2.5 oz [[almond flour]]
- 1/2 cup [[lemon juice]]
- 1/2 cup [[vegetable oil]]
- 1/4 cup [[water]]
- 3/4 tsp [[garlic powder]]
- 1/2 tsp [[ground coriander]]
- 1/2 tsp [[dried sage]]
- 3/4 tsp [[dried oregano]]
- 15g [[nutritional yeast]]
- 3/4 tsp [[kosher salt]]
- ## Method
- 1. If using canned chickpeas, combine the chickpeas, baking soda, and 3 cups of water in a medium pot. Bring to a boil and cook for 2 minutes.
  2. Drain the chickpeas in a colander. Remove and discard as many skins as possible using a spoon, fork, or by rubbing them gently. Let cool for at least 5 minutes.
  3. If using chickpea flour instead of whole chickpeas, skip the boiling and skin removal steps.
  4. Add the chickpeas (or chickpea flour), almond flour, lemon juice, vegetable oil, water, garlic powder, ground coriander, dried sage, dried oregano, nutritional yeast, and kosher salt to a blender or food processor.
  5. Blend until completely smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed.
  6. Adjust consistency with a small amount of additional water if necessary.
  7. Serve immediately or refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 1 week.
